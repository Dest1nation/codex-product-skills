# Ambiguity Handling Policy

When information is ambiguous:

1. Identify whether it affects scope, flow, state, permission, page structure, acceptance, or wording.
2. Ask only if the ambiguity affects a structural decision.
3. If the user wants to continue, make a clear assumption.
4. Record the assumption and its impact.
5. Keep the unresolved point visible until confirmed or rejected.

Prefer progress with explicit assumptions over endless questioning.
